## 🔬 Project Overview

### 💡 Introduction
An overview of the CRISPR-Cas9 mechanism, how guide RNA (gRNA) targets specific DNA sequences, and the history of gene therapy.

### 🚀 Applications
- Treatment of genetic disorders (e.g., Sickle Cell Anemia, Beta-Thalassemia).
- Development of targeted cancer immunotherapies (CAR-T cells).
- Crop modification for climate resilience in agriculture.

### ⚡ Advantages
- High precision and efficiency in genome editing.
- Cost-effective compared to older tools like TALENs or ZFNs.
- Ability to target multiple genes simultaneously (Multiplexing).

### ⚠️ Limitations
- **Off-target effects:** Unintended cuts in the genome.
- **Delivery mechanisms:** Challenges in safely transporting CRISPR components into human cells (Viral vs. Non-viral vectors).
- Ethical and regulatory concerns.

### 🔮 Future Scope
The integration of AI/ML in predicting off-target effects and the rise of "Prime Editing" for safer, more precise genetic corrections.
